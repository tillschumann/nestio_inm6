\abstract{NEST \cite{NEST} is a simulator for spiking neural
  networks. It runs on all systems from local machines to
  supercomputers \cite{Plesser07}. Storing simulation data efficiently
  is essential for neuroscientific studies, but not trivial on
  supercomputers with centralized storage.

  Virtual devices store neuronal properties such as spiking activity
  or membrane voltages to disk. With increasing numbers of devices and
  compute nodes, writing becomes a bottleneck and new I/O strategies
  are required.

  To assess different I/O strategies and libraries, we implemented a
  \emph{proxy}, which imitates the writing behavior of NEST based on a
  few parameters (e.g. number of nerve cells recorded, number of
  threads, recording or spike rate).

  The proxy allows benchmarking and statistical analysis and
  consecutively optimizations without the complexity of running full
  NEST simulations. The results of the benchmarks will guide our way
  towards a new I/O sub-system for NEST.}
